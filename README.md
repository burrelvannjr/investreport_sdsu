## Course Description

The ability to analyze and interpret quantitative data, conduct research independently, and effectively present ideas in a coherent and convincing way are highly regarded skills on the job market. By the conclusion of this course, students will have developed and demonstrated effective research, analytical, critical thinking, problem solving, and communication skills.

Students will expand their skills by learning how to analyze and interpret data, use effective data visualization techniques, and report their analyses in a professional format. Students will also read and discuss research relevant to the field of criminal justice.

This course is offered in an online/asynchronous format and is organized into 8 modules. Within each module, students read chapters from the course reader, watch videos, and complete assignments for credit. All assignments are due on Saturdays at 11:59 PM of their assigned weeks.


## Course Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Use basic data collection and analysis techniques relevant to the field of criminal justice
2. Identify organizational or policy problems and present relevant context
3. Analyze empirical data collected by law enforcement organizations
4. Produce graphs, figures, and tables
5. Synthesize methodological knowledge and data on a selected topic into an executive summary for an agency report, using proper syntax, grammar, and correctly cited sources

## Assignments

### Course Engagement (25 pts)
Regular, consistent engagement with course content throughout the semester. In this asynchronous format, engagement is assessed through Canvas logins and access to weekly course materials.

### Prep Assignments (30 pts — 5 pts each)
Six prep assignments distributed across the semester, each designed to help students practice data presentation and analysis skills. One prep is due approximately every few weeks.

### Quizzes (15 pts — 5 pts each)
Three quizzes assessing understanding of data analysis concepts. Quizzes are open book but are **not** collaborative.

### Discussion Posts (15 pts — 5 pts each)
Three discussion board posts in which students read assigned academic articles and respond to posted questions, engaging actively with criminal justice research.

### Final Executive Summary (5 pts)
A 2–5 page summary analyzing issues confronted in police departments using provided data, written in professional report style with proper citation.

### Final Recorded Presentation (10 pts)
A 10-minute recorded PowerPoint presentation covering all aspects of the findings from the Executive Summary, uploaded to Canvas.
